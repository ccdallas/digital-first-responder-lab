2025–2026 Intelligence Trends
Shift to Vendor Targeting
Attacks on healthcare businesses — vendors, MSPs, billing providers — surged 30% in 2025. Threat actors discovered that targeting a single vendor creates cascading disruption across hundreds of provider organizations simultaneously.
Vulnerability Exploitation Overtakes Credentials
For the first time in three years, exploited vulnerabilities (33%) surpassed credential-based attacks as the leading technical root cause — overtaking phishing and stolen credentials that dominated 2023–2024.
Top Three Threat Groups
Interlock, Qilin (GOLD FEATHER), and RansomHub (GOLD HUBBARD) dominated healthcare targeting in 2025 based on leak site observations. INC Ransom led by incident volume; Interlock led by total records stolen.
2026 Emerging Threat
Health sector security professionals identify AI-enabled attacks as the leading threat for 2026 — surpassing ransomware for the first time. Zero-day exploits and third-party breaches follow. The attack surface is expanding faster than defenses.
Dwell Time Remains Critical
The Kettering Health attack illustrated the danger: network breached April 9 — attack triggered May 20. Six weeks of undetected dwell time. Early detection remains the single most impactful defensive investment available to healthcare organizations.
DFR Framework Gap
No current framework adequately addresses the clinical continuity dimension of ransomware — what happens to patients during downtime. HIPAA, NIST, and HHS 405(d) all focus on data protection, not care delivery continuity during active incidents. This is the gap this lab exists to document.
