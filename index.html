Core Philosophy

At 2am in the ER, I'm not waiting on a dashboard. Healthcare doesn't pause for data validation. Our security frameworks need to stop assuming it does.

Most healthcare cybersecurity focuses on protecting data. This lab focuses on what happens when the technology keeping a person alive fails — in real time, under real conditions, with real consequences.

The scenarios here are not hypothetical. They are built from 28+ years of clinical emergency medicine, interventional radiology, hyperbarics, and pediatric care — and from being a device-dependent patient myself.

Failure Scenarios
01 / SCENARIO
Silent Battery Failure
Portable O₂ Concentrator
Critical

Battery indicator reports full charge. Device loses power within minutes of disconnection. Patient has no backup oxygen source. Power infrastructure is unstable.

Failure Chain

1
Device reports false state. Battery management system shows full charge. Actual runtime: 2–3 minutes.
2
Patient trusts the indicator. No manual verification protocol exists. No alert threshold configured.
3
Power event occurs. Grid outage, storm, or infrastructure failure disconnects AC power.
4
Battery depletes in under 3 minutes. No backup tank. No secondary concentrator. No escalation path.
5
Patient enters oxygen deprivation. PAH + hypoxia. Cascading physiological failure begins silently.

Framework Gaps Identified

NIST 800-53 SI-13 (Predictable Failure) NIST AI RMF MANAGE 2.2 HIPAA § 164.310(a)(2)(i) FDA 21 CFR Part 820 IEC 62443-4-2 CR 3.1 Home Care Gap: No Redundancy Standard

What should exist: Real-time battery health telemetry with verified discharge testing, mandatory backup protocols for single-source oxygen-dependent patients, infrastructure dependency mapping for high-risk home medical devices, and emergency registry enrollment at the local utility and fire department level.

02 / SCENARIO
AI Triage Override
Emergency Department
Critical

AI-assisted triage system flags a patient as low acuity. Clinician overrides based on clinical observation. System logs the override as anomalous. Alert fatigue follows.

Failure Chain

1
AI model scores patient: low risk. Model trained on structured EHR data. Misses nonverbal cues, skin color change, work of breathing.
2
Clinician trusts observation over score. Escalates immediately. Patient is actually critical.
3
System flags clinician override as deviation. Algorithm treats experienced clinical judgment as an error signal.
4
Override data used to retrain model. Model learns to suppress clinical intuition patterns. Future accuracy degrades silently.
5
Next clinician defers to AI. Alert fatigue + institutional trust in system. Patient harm occurs.

Framework Gaps Identified

NIST AI RMF GOVERN 1.1 NIST AI RMF MAP 3.5 FDA AI/ML Action Plan 2021 ONC HTI-1 Rule Human Oversight Gap Feedback Loop Risk

What should exist: Clinical override logging that distinguishes expert judgment from error, model retraining governance that requires human review before incorporating override data, mandatory human-in-the-loop thresholds for high-acuity clinical AI systems, and regular red-team exercises that test AI performance against experienced clinician baseline decisions.

03 / SCENARIO
Infusion Pump
Network Compromise
High

Networked infusion pump receives unauthorized parameter update during active medication delivery. Nurse administering care is managing four other patients simultaneously.

Failure Chain

1
Pump connected to clinical network. Drug library update mechanism lacks mutual authentication. Lateral movement from prior breach.
2
Unauthorized rate parameter pushed. Change falls within acceptable range. No immediate alarm triggered.
3
Nurse managing four patients. Controlled chaos. Visual check interval is 20+ minutes. No bedside alert.
4
Patient receives incorrect dose over time. Subtle presentation. Clinical deterioration begins before detection.
5
Root cause is IT investigation, not clinical. 6–8 hours to identify source. Evidence degraded. Incident not reported to FDA.

Framework Gaps Identified

NIST 800-82 Rev 3 (OT Security) FDA Cybersecurity Guidance 2023 HIPAA § 164.312(b) Audit Controls IEC 80001-1 Risk Management Network Segmentation Gap MDS² Documentation Gap

What should exist: Mutual TLS authentication for all pump parameter updates, real-time anomaly detection on infusion rate changes against prescription baseline, clinical workflow-aware alerting that accounts for nurse-to-patient ratios, mandatory FDA MedWatch reporting integration, and post-incident forensic preservation protocols for IoMT devices.
